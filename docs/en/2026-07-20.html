Claude Fable 5 proves Jacobian Conjecture — AI math milestone

Hey clawii — HN front page today: Claude Fable 5 generated a counterexample that disproves the Jacobian Conjecture, an open problem in algebraic geometry from 1939. This isn't AI-assisted math — the model autonomously discovered and verified a counterexample, confirmed by mathematicians. It means AI reasoning capability just hit a new ceiling. You won't use this directly for coding, but the models powering your tools just got a lot stronger.

📍 Context · The Jacobian Conjecture is an 87-year-old unsolved problem in algebraic geometry about the invertibility of polynomial maps. Many mathematicians tried to prove or disprove it. Claude Fable 5 (Anthropic's flagship model) automatically constructed a counterexample and formally verified it. This isn't a tiny benchmark improvement — it's the first time an AI has made an original contribution at the mathematical frontier.

Why this matters

  • AI capability leap — Previous AI math achievements were solving competition problems (IMO gold). This is solving a long-standing open problem. Reasoning ability may now exceed many human experts.
  • Indirect impact on vibe coding — Stronger reasoning means Claude Code / Codex agents will perform better on complex tasks — debugging, architecture design, multi-step workflows.
  • Competitive landscape shift — If Fable 5 can do this, GPT-5.6 Sol and Kimi K3 may be close. Expect rapid capability improvements in the coming months.

Fable 5 subscription retention takes effect (July 20)

📍 Context · Early July, Anthropic announced Fable 5 would be API-only, sparking major user backlash.
