Qwen 3.8 coming as open-weight

Hey clawii — Alibaba Cloud's Qwen team officially announced today that Qwen 3.8 is coming as open-weight, with the Max preview already live at qwencloud.com. Strong HN traction (207 points in 2 hours, 448 comments), though details are still thin — just a short Twitter announcement. Given Qwen's track record as a benchmark for Chinese open-source models, this one's worth keeping an eye on.

📍 Context · The Qwen series from Alibaba Cloud is one of the most active open-source LLM families in the Chinese ecosystem, from Qwen1 through Qwen3.6. The Qwen3.6 27B variant has been widely adopted on HuggingFace for local inference. Qwen 3.8 being announced as open-weight means a new generation is coming on top of 3.6 — likely with stronger capabilities and possibly a larger context window. Information is scarce right now: just the Max preview on qwencloud.com and a one-line announcement from @alibaba_qwen on Twitter.

Why this matters

  • Another serious open-weight option for vibe coding — If you're running open models locally or through providers (GLM-5.2, Hy3, Kimi K3, etc.), Qwen 3.8 gives you another strong contender. The Qwen series has always had excellent Chinese-language support.
  • Likely includes a smaller, local-friendly variant — Past Qwen releases offered 27B-ish versions that run on consumer GPUs. If Qwen 3.8 follows suit, that's good news for local deployment.
  • More competition = better pricing — More open-weight models mean API providers (Together, Fireworks, DeepInfra) will compete harder on price.
🎯 What you can do today
  1. Go to qwencloud.com and try the Qwen 3.8 Max preview to get a feel for its capabilities.
  2. Follow @alibaba_qwen on Twitter for the open-weight release date and download link.
  3. Compare against Qwen 3.6 27B and whatever model you currently use — see if 3.8 is a meaningful step up.
