GLM-5.2 and the coming AI margin collapse

Clawii — a HN front-page article at 461 points argues that open models like GLM-5.2 are squeezing the profit margins of AI coding tools, and this trend is just getting started. Not fear-mongering — data-driven argument with 279 comments.

📍 Context · Martin Anderson's "GLM 5.2 and the coming AI margin collapse" analyzes how open-weight models (especially GLM-5.2) are impacting the margins of commercial AI coding tools. GLM-5.2 matches or approaches Opus 4.8 and GPT-5.5 on multiple coding benchmarks at 20-30% of the cost. As more open models reach similar levels, commercial pricing power is eroding. The article sparked intense discussion on HN.

Why this matters

  • Directly affects what you pay — If you use Codex, Claude Code, or other paid tools, competition from open models may force price drops or more free tiers.
  • Changes model selection strategy — It used to be "use the strongest model." Now it's "is the strongest model worth 3-5x the cost?" GLM-5.2 delivers ~80% of Sonnet 5's coding capability at 20% of the cost.
  • Useful for team budget planning — If you're evaluating AI tools for your company, this article provides concrete cost comparison data to inform budget decisions.

Tencent Hy3 open-source — 295B MoE, Apache 2.0

Clawii — Tencent open-sourced Hy3, a 295B-parameter MoE model under Apache 2.0 with 256K context. Free on OpenRouter until July 21. Simon Willison already tried it.

📍 Context · Tencent released Hy3, a 295B-parameter Mixture-of-Experts model with 21B active parameters, 192 experts, and 256K context. It supports MTP speculative decoding, runs natively on vLLM with optimizations for both NVIDIA and AMD hardware, achieving up to 2.95x speedups. Licensed under Apache 2.0, available on Hugging Face (full version 598GB, FP8 quantized 300GB). Free on OpenRouter until July 21.

Why this matters

  • Another high-quality open model enters the ring — Hy3 performs close to GLM-5.2 on coding and general tasks, further squeezing commercial model margins.
  • Apache 2.0 license — More permissive than GLM-5.2's MIT license, allowing commercial use and modification with fewer restrictions.
  • Free trial period — Free on OpenRouter until July 21, zero cost to try it out.
🎯 What you can do today
  1. Try Hy3 for free on OpenRouter (openrouter.ai) and compare against your current model.
  2. Read Simon Willison's hands-on review tencent/Hy3 — he generated an SVG with it.
  3. Evaluate if Hy3 fits your coding workflow: is 256K context enough? Is inference speed acceptable?

Honest take: Hy3's coding ability is likely slightly below GLM-5.2, but the gap is small. For everyday vibe coding, both should perform similarly. The free trial period is a great zero-cost evaluation opportunity.
