GPT-5.5 hallucinates 3x more than MIT-licensed GLM-5.2 — a comparison worth reading

A head-to-head comparison from arrowtsx.dev hit HN with 202 points and 61 comments. The core finding: GPT-5.5's hallucination rate is 3x higher than GLM-5.2, an MIT-licensed open model from Z.ai. This challenges the common assumption that closed-source models are more reliable.

📍 Context

GLM-5.2 is Z.ai's recent MIT-licensed open-weight model — 753B parameters (40B active MoE), 1M token context window, ranking #3 on FrontierSWE and #1 on Design Arena. GPT-5.5 is OpenAI's flagship. This comparison systematically evaluates hallucination rates, and the results flip a common assumption about open vs closed models.

Why this matters

  • Challenges "closed is more reliable" — Many assume paid closed models (GPT-5.5, Claude Opus) are inherently more reliable than open ones. This test shows that on hallucination — a key quality metric — the open model wins.
  • What this means for you — If you use AI for accuracy-sensitive tasks (data analysis, document generation), hallucination rate is a key metric. GLM-5.2 might be a more reliable choice for text-only work.
  • Open model competitiveness is real — GLM-5.2's MIT license means free use, modification, and commercial deployment. For enterprises, that matters.
🎯 Action — what you can do today
  1. Read the original post — arrowtsx.dev's comparison article. See the full methodology and results.
  2. Read the HN discussion — HN thread (202 pts, 61 comments). See the community's take on hallucination rates.
  3. Try GLM-5.2 for yourself — Access it via OpenRouter or HuggingFace. Compare its output quality against GPT-5.5 on your own tasks.

Test methodology — The article used a standardized hallucination test suite covering factual Q&A, document summarization, code generation, and other tasks. Results showed GPT-5.5 producing significantly more hallucinations (inaccurate or fabricated information) across multiple task categories.

Why GLM-5.2 might hallucinate less — Possible explanations: 1) GLM-5.2's training data may emphasize factual accuracy; 2) Its IndexShare sparse attention architecture may help reduce hallucination; 3) Its 1M token context window allows better context understanding.

What this means for you — 1) For accuracy-sensitive text tasks (data analysis, document generation), GLM-5.2 may be more reliable; 2) But GLM-5.2 is text-only — no multimodal support; 3) If you need multimodal (image understanding), GPT-5.5 is still the better choice.

Honest caveat — 1) This is a single independent comparison, not an official benchmark; 2) Hallucination rate is just one quality dimension, not overall performance; 3) GLM-5.2 needs GPU hardware for self-deployment.

🔭 What to watch for next

1) Whether more independent tests validate this finding; 2) Whether OpenAI responds; 3) Whether GLM-5.2 gets a multimodal variant.

Hyundai buys Boston Dynamics — robotics market reshuffles

Hyundai announced the acquisition of Boston Dynamics today. HN 838 pts, 365 comments. This is a major robotics industry consolidation that could reshape humanoid and industrial robot development for years.

📍 Context

Boston Dynamics is the world's most famous robotics company — known for Atlas (humanoid), Spot (quadruped), and their viral videos. Previously owned by SoftBank, the company is now being acquired by Hyundai, South Korea's largest automaker. This signals a shift from lab research to industrial-scale deployment of advanced robotics.

Why this matters

  • Major robotics industry consolidation — Boston Dynamics' core technology (Atlas humanoid robot, Spot quadruped) moves from SoftBank's portfolio to Hyundai's industrial ecosystem. This likely accelerates industrial deployment.
  • What this means for you — Even if you don't work with robots directly, this acquisition signals that humanoid robots are getting closer to real-world factory and warehouse use. That could reshape manufacturing, logistics, and eventually service industries.
  • Hyundai's AI strategy — Hyundai is transforming from an automaker into a smart mobility company. This acquisition is a key piece of their AI + robotics playbook.
🎯 Action — what you can do today
  1. Read the HN discussion — HN thread (838 pts, 365 comments). See the community's take on the acquisition.
  2. Follow official announcements — Watch Hyundai and Boston Dynamics for product roadmap updates post-acquisition.
  3. Think about application scenarios — If you work in manufacturing or logistics, consider how humanoid robots might change workflows in the next 3-5 years.
