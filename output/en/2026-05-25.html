KPMG announced on 5/19, PwC on 5/14. As of today (5/25), six days have passed without a third announcement. This is normal procurement velocity — and it tells you that your company's IT window just compressed from "by year-end" to "this quarter."

📍 Context

Two of the four — including the most conservative ones — deploying Claude in the same month signals that enterprise AI adoption is moving from "pilot phase" into "standardization phase." The 6-day gap since KPMG isn't unusual: Deloitte and EY are deep in diligence — migration costs, compliance audits, pilot reviews. A 2-3 week gap between Big-4 announcements is standard for this level of procurement.

The "we should look into this" conversation is now imminent, not hypothetical.
  • For your comp/HR work: by the time your firm standardizes on Claude (or GPT Enterprise, or both), you want to already be the person who knows how these tools handle compensation data, policy analysis, and market benchmarking. The runway is 2-3 months, not 6.
  • Deloitte is the bellwether now. They're the largest Big-4 by revenue. If Deloitte picks Claude, the signal is irreversible. If they pick GPT Enterprise, suddenly it's a horse race. Watch the next 14 days.

👀 Worth a Look · Long-Context New Baseline

Gemini 3.5 Flash — 1M Context, One Week In, Worth Trying Now

Google I/O on 5/18 released Gemini 3.5 Flash with a 1M-token context window. Seven days later, there's enough user feedback to judge whether it's useful for you. It is — if you regularly handle long documents. Try feeding it a compensation report.

📍 Context

1M tokens of context ≈ roughly 800,000 words, or an 800-page book. For comparison: Claude Sonnet 4.6 handles 200K, GPT-5.5 handles 256K. Google just pushed the boundary to the million-token level. For you (a non-developer doing long-document analysis), this means: instead of splitting a long file into 3-5 chunks and feeding it to an AI piece by piece, you can dump the entire thing in one go and ask questions. An annual compensation survey, internal HR manual, and industry benchmark report — all at once: "Where are we lagging vs the market?"

One week of early feedback: the 1M context is real and usable (not just a spec-sheet number), but retrieval precision in the middle portion of very long documents still needs improvement.

Signal Analysis

Why this matters — one week in

  • 1M context is real, not just a spec sheet number. Early user reports confirm that Gemini 3.5 Flash can handle book-length inputs without catastrophic forgetting at the start — a genuine problem with earlier long-context models. This is the first widely-available model that actually works at this scale.
  • Interesting trade-off: breadth vs depth. Gemini Flash is weaker on deep reasoning than Claude Sonnet 4.6 or Opus 4.7. But for "find the paragraph about X across 600 pages and summarize it," it's actually the best tool because it sees everything at once.
  • Your optimal split strategy is emerging:
    · Read + find + summarize (low reasoning, high recall) → Gemini 3.5 Flash
    · Analyze + argue + write (high reasoning) → Claude Sonnet/Opus
    · Meeting notes + video/audio → Gemini Omni
  • Cost matters for daily use. Flash is designed to be roughly 1/10 the cost of flagship models. If you process long documents regularly, the price difference adds up fast.
🎯 Action — what you can do this week
  1. Install the Gemini CLI: brew install gemini-cli or npm install -g @google-gemini/cli. The free tier is enough for 10-20 long-document analyses.
  2. Pick a long file you'd normally read: Recommended order — (1) annual compensation survey report, (2) labor law full text, (3) your company's HR policy manual. Run it through Gemini 3.5 Flash with a query like "find all provisions about X and compare across chapters."
  3. A/B test it: Same document, same questions, run through Claude Sonnet 4.6. Compare the two results — which is more complete, which is more accurate, which cites better. Write down your findings. That's your personal routing guide.
  4. If your document has tables or numbers: Pay special attention to whether Gemini accurately reads table structure and cross-page numbers. This is a classic weak point for long-context models. If you see problems, tell me.

Honest caveat: 1M context is the ceiling, not the norm. Above 500K tokens, response speed slows noticeably and retrieval precision can drift. Google itself positions Flash as "high-frequency, cheap, good enough" — not "strongest reasoning." For you, the sweet spot is: a 300+ page PDF, some quick Q&A, no need for deep analysis. Deep work still goes to Claude.

Consistent with last week's Reasonix split logic: different tasks → different models. The question now isn't "which model is best" but "when to use which."

🔭 Watch — what to look for next

Watch whether Anthropic and OpenAI match the 1M context bar by June 15. If they do → long-context standard rises, price war begins. If they don't → Google has a real lead in context length (potentially lasting through year-end), and you should seriously consider shifting your long-document work to Gemini.
